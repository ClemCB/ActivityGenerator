What is the Independent Visitor Scheme?

Independent Visitors (IVs) are volunteers who befriend young people during their time in care. Volunteers need no formal qualifications, just a genuine and active interest in the welfare of children and young people.

Action For Children organise the London Independent Visitor Scheme and respective local councils coordinate the scheme throughout the rest of the UK. The volunteer mentors guide, advise, listen to and befriend young people, sharing their experiences as well as trying new activities and spending quality time together.

IV responsibilities include:

  • Monthly (minimum) face to face meetings with the young people with a two year minimum commitment in the role
  • A willingness, where appropriate, to travel in London and the South East to meet the young person
  • Accompanying the young person to access hobbies and interests e.g. cinema trips, cycle rides, tenpin bowling and anything you are both up for!
  • Providing emotional and practical support where appropriate
  • Assisting the young person to work towards making decisions about their future service needs where appropriate
  • Completing brief logs of each visit
  • Keeping receipts for all activities/travelcosts during each visit for the purpose of claiming expenses and safeguarding
  • Attending quarterly supervision meetings with your dedicated volunteer coordinator
